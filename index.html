Rugby

What is Rugby?

Rugby is a high-energy ball game that combines strength, speed, and strategy.
Played with an oval-shaped ball, it is one of the most physically demanding sports in the world.
The game has two main versions: Rugby Union and Rugby League.

Where did Rugby come from?

Rugby’s history dates back to 1823 in England.
Legend says a student named William Webb Ellis picked up the ball during a football match and ran with it.
This act led to the creation of a new sport. Today, rugby is played globally,
with major tournaments like the Rugby World Cup.

Basic rules of Rugby

Rugby has simple but strict rules. The objective is to score more points than the opponent. Points can be earned through tries, conversions, penalties, and drop goals.

1. Scoring in Rugby
Try (5 points): Scored by grounding the ball in the opponent’s in-goal area.
Conversion (2 points): A kick after a try, taken in line with where the try was scored.
Penalty Kick (3 points): Awarded after certain fouls.
Drop Goal (3 points): Scored by kicking the ball through the goalposts during open play.

2. Passing and Tackling
Players can only pass the ball backward or sideways.
Forward passes result in a scrum for the opposing team. Tackling is allowed but must be below the shoulders.
Dangerous tackles lead to penalties or suspensions.

3. Set Pieces: Scrums and Lineouts
Scrum: Used to restart play after minor infringements. Eight forwards from each team bind together and push for the ball.
Lineout: Occurs when the ball goes out of play. Players lift teammates to catch the thrown-in ball.
4. Offside Rule
Players must stay behind the ball carrier. Being in front of the ball without retreating is offside and results in a penalty.
